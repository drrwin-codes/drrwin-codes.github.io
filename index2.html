ICML Workshop · 2025 Graph Learning
How Sequential Is Your Graph? A New Lens for Long-Range Graph Learning

We introduce Hamiltonian width, a graph-theoretic measure of how sequential a graph's structure is, and use it to design PC-PE (Path Cover Positional Encoding) for Graph Transformers. We prove that standard message-passing networks require O(d) layers for d-long-range tasks, while GPS with PC-PE reduces this to O(1) when nodes share a backbone path — a claim we test and support via stratified LRGB benchmarks and a six-condition ablation suite.

Prospective · AAAS 2027 Drug Discovery
QSAR Modeling of LDHA Inhibitors: Ensembles vs. Transformers for Potency Prediction

LDHA, an enzyme central to tumor metabolism, is a validated oncological target with no inhibitor yet reaching clinical trials. We benchmark transformer-based tabular architectures (TabTransformer, FT-Transformer, SAINT) against stacked ensemble models for pIC50 prediction on 554 curated ChEMBL compounds. Scaffold-aware splitting reveals meaningful generalization gaps, and SHAP analysis identifies molecular drug-likeness as the primary driver of inhibitor potency.
